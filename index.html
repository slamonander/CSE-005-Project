DATA AND SYSTEM DISRUPTORS

  • RANSOMWARE: Disables victim's access to data through encryption until a ransom is paid.
  • WIPER MALWARE: Erases user data and ensures the data cannot be covered. Used to take down computer networks in companies as well as cover up traces left after an intrusion.
  • VIRUSES: Steals data, launch DDoS attacks, or installs ransomware. The only difference from a virus to a worm and Trojan is that it needs the infected application to run.
  • WORMS: Accesses the system through backdoors built into the software, through software vulnerabilities, or through flash drives. Worms can also launch DDoS attacks, steal data, or launch ransomware attacks.

INFORMATION THEFT

  • SPYWARE: Collects data activity without the user's knowledge.
  • KEYLOGGERS: Monitors user's activity. Can steal password data, banking information, and other sensitive information. Can be inserted into a system through phishing, social engineering, or malicious downloads.
  • TROJANS: Disguises itself as a legitimate code, which then takes control of the user's systems.
  • MOBILE MALWARE: Distributed through phishing and malicious downloads. Jailbroken phones are especially vulnerable to these attacks due to the lack of protection from the device's original OS.

NUISANCE AND ADVERTISING MALWARE

  • ADWARE: Tracks the user's activity to determine the advertisement served to them. This means the user's activity is collected to create a profile of said user to share or sell to advertisers without user's consent.
  • FILELESS MALWARE: Changes files that are native to the operating system, making the attacks more successful since the OS recognizes these files as legitimate.
