W A R E S

1. DATA AND SYSTEM DISRUPTORS

Malwares that are designed to damage/encrypt/steal data, disrupt a computer's system, and demand ransom while holding the user's data hostage.

01. RANSOMWARE

Ransomware diables victim's access to data through encryption until a ransom is paid.

02. WIPER MALWARE

Wiper malware erases the user's data and ensures that the data cannot be recovered. This is used to take down computer networks in companies as well as cover up traces left after an intrusion.

03. VIRUSES

Viruses steals data, launches DDoS attacks, or installs ransomware. The only difference from a virus to a worm and Trojans is that it needs the infected application to run.

04. WORMS

Worms access the system through backdoors built into the software, through software vulnerabilities, or through flash drives. Worms can also launch DDoS attacks, steal data, or launch ransomware attacks.

2. INFORMATION THEFT

Malwares used to capture and monitor the user's activities, steal data, and compromise systems on devices.

01. SPYWARE

Spyware collects data activity without the user's knowledge.

02. KEYLOGGERS

Keyloggers monitor the user's activity and can steal password data, banking information, and other sensitive information. Keyloggers can be inserted into a system through phishing, social engineering, or malicious downloads.

03. TROJANS

Trojans can disguise itself as legitimate code, which then takes control of the user's systems.

04. MOBILE MALWARE:

Mobile malware can be distributed through phishing and malicious downloads. Jailbroken phones are especially vulnerable to these attacks due to the lack of protection from the device's original OS.

3. NUISANCE AND ADVERTISING MALWARE

Malwares that bombards users with unwanted advertisement, compromise and take control of systems, and/or exploits users.

01. ADWARE

Adware tracks the user's activity to determine the advertisement to serve to them. This means that the user's activity is collected to create a profile of said user to share or sell to advertisers without user's consent.

02. FILELESS MALWARE

Fileless malware changes the files that are native to the operating system, making the attacks more successful since the OS recognize these files as legitimate.

03. BOTS

A bot is a software that can perform automated tasks. Many bots, or botnet, can launch a broad flood of attacks, like DDoS attacks.

04. ROOTKITS

A rootkit is a software that can remotely control a user's computer with full admin privileges. Rootkits can be installed into applications, kernels, hypervisors, or firmware. It spreads through phishing, malicious attachments, downloads, and compromised share drives. Rootkits are also used to conceal other malwares.
